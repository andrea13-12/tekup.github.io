Ceviche is perhaps Peru's most famous dish, consisting of fresh raw fish or seafood marinated in freshly squeezed lime or bitter orange juice, with sliced onions, chili peppers, and cilantro. The acidity of the citrus juice "cooks" the seafood, giving it a unique texture and flavor. It's typically served with sweet potato, corn, and lettuce. Ceviche represents the coastal influence in Peruvian cuisine and showcases the country's abundant seafood.

Lomo Saltado is a popular stir-fry dish that reflects Peru's Chinese culinary influence. It combines strips of beef (lomo), tomatoes, onions, and peppers stir-fried in a hot wok with soy sauce and vinegar. It's often served with French fries and rice, creating a fusion of flavors and textures that is both hearty and satisfying.

Anticuchos are skewers of marinated and grilled beef heart, a dish that dates back to pre-Columbian times and remains a beloved street food in Peru. The marinade typically includes aji panca (a mild Peruvian chili pepper), garlic, cumin, and vinegar, giving the meat a smoky, savory flavor. Anticuchos are often served with boiled potatoes and a spicy Peruvian sauce called aji sauce.
